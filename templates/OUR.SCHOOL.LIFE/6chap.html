CHAPTER 6

Neighborhood Chores and Unexpected Company

rarely left her house unless necessary. It wasn’t that she disliked being outside—she just preferred peace and quiet. Unfortunately, peace wasn’t an option today. Her mother had asked her to run errands around the neighborhood, which meant interacting with people. Worst of all, she had to walk past Ulysses’ house. She prayed he wouldn’t be outside.

She lost that prayer immediately.

The moment she stepped outside, Ulysses was already there, leaning against his front gate. He had a plastic bag in one hand, a drink in the other, and a familiar smirk on his face.

“Leighton, what a surprise,” he called. “Did you miss me already?”

She ignored him and kept walking.

“Oh? No response? That hurts.”

He easily caught up, matching her pace.

“Where are you headed?”
“Nowhere.”
“Wow, what a coincidence. I’m going nowhere too.”

She sighed deeply.

“I have errands.”
“Oh? Need help?”
“No.”
“Too bad. I’m coming anyway.”

First Stop: Mrs. Cruz’s Garden

Her first stop was Mrs. Cruz’s garden. The old lady had asked for some groceries, and Leighton was supposed to drop them off. She knocked quietly on the gate.

“Coming, dear!”

Mrs. Cruz’s cheerful voice rang out. The moment she opened the gate and saw Ulysses, she grinned knowingly.

“Oh my, you two are together again?”

Leighton’s face turned red.

“It’s not like that,” she muttered.

Ulysses grinned.

“She can’t get rid of me.”
“Young love is wonderful,” Mrs. Cruz teased.

“We’re not—”

Leighton tried to protest, but Mrs. Cruz was already laughing.

As soon as they left, Ulysses leaned close.

“So, should I start calling you my fiancée?”

She shoved him.

“Shut up.”

He chuckled.

Second Stop: The Market

Leighton had a list of things to buy, and she wanted to get in and out quickly. But Ulysses had other plans. He stopped at almost every stall, asking pointless questions.

“Leighton, do you think I’d look cool wearing sunglasses?”
“No.”
“What about a hat?”
“No.”
“A cape?”

She glared.

“Are you buying anything or just being annoying?”
“Both,” he admitted.

At the fruit stall, an old vendor smiled at them.

“A lovely young couple, huh?”

Leighton almost dropped the apples.

“We’re not a couple,” she muttered.

Ulysses casually slung an arm around her shoulder.

“She’s just shy.”

Leighton’s entire body stiffened. The vendor laughed.

“Ah, young love.”

She stomped on Ulysses’ foot.

“Ow! Okay, okay, I’ll stop,” he chuckled.

Once they finally left the market, Leighton let out a sigh of relief.

“That was torture.”
“For you,” Ulysses corrected. “I had fun.”

She glared at him. He just grinned.

Last Stop: The Bakery

Leighton had been sent to pick up bread, but as soon as she stepped inside, Ulysses vanished. She frowned, looking around.

“Where did he—”

“Leighton.”

She jumped as he suddenly appeared behind her, holding two trays of pastries.

“You have to try these.”
“I don’t—”
“Too late. I’m buying them.”

Minutes later, they were sitting outside, eating pastries. Leighton stared at hers, unsure. Ulysses nudged her.

“Just eat.”

She hesitated before taking a small bite. It was… good.

Ulysses smirked.

“See? I have good taste.”

She didn’t reply, but she took another bite. That was answer enough.

Heading Home

By the time they returned to her house, the sun was already setting. Leighton was exhausted. Ulysses, of course, looked completely fine.

“Today was fun,” he said. “Let’s do it again sometime.”

She stared at him.

“No.”

He chuckled.

“You’ll change your mind.”

As she stepped inside, she heard him call out one last thing.

“Sweet dreams, Leighton!”

She slammed the door shut.

Through the window, she could see him laughing.
