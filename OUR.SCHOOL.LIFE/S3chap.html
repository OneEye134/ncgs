Secret Chapter: The Ride from Hell

Leighton should have known.

She should have known that following Ulysses anywhere was a mistake.

And yet, here she was.

Strapped into what could only be described as a death machine in disguise.

The ride loomed above them like a metallic beast, its massive LED sign flickering with an ominous message:

“THE SCREAM REAPER – 5-TIME WINNER OF ‘WORLD’S SCARIEST RIDE’”

The Warning Signs

At first, Leighton thought it was just a normal roller coaster. Terrifying, sure. But nothing she couldn’t handle.

Then, she noticed something.

  • The staff at the entrance? Smirking.
  • The people getting off the ride? Shaking. Pale. Some looked like they had seen something—something that had broken them.

Leighton took a step back.

“Absolutely not.”

Ulysses placed a hand on her back.

“What’s the worst that could happen?”

Leighton glared at him.

“You literally don’t know what this ride does.”

He grinned.

“Exactly. Which makes it more fun.”

She turned to their friends for support.

  • Christian? Laughing.
  • Fritz? Recording.
  • Val and Leonard? Hyping it up.
  • Yeshe and Daniel? Already in line.

Leighton? Betrayed.

The Ride Begins

And that was how Leighton found herself trapped in the front row, her hands gripping the harness so tightly her knuckles turned white.

Ulysses, sitting beside her, smirked.

“You ready?”

“No.”

Too late.

“WELCOME TO THE SCREAM REAPER.”

“SURVIVAL RATE: CLASSIFIED.”
“PREPARE FOR… THE UNKNOWN.”

A deep rumbling sound echoed around them.

The ride jolted.

And then—

The floor beneath them disappeared.

WHOOSH—

They plummeted into pure darkness.

Leighton’s scream never even made it out.

For a full five seconds, they free-fell in total silence. No wind. No sound. Just the feeling of absolute nothingness.

And then—

"HELLO."

A face—A FACE—suddenly jumped out of the darkness, inches from her own.

Leighton nearly died on the spot.

The ride jerked violently, shooting them into a narrow tunnel filled with flashing lights and horrifying whispers.

Ulysses let out a choked sound.

“Wha—WHAT IS THIS?!”

Leighton was too busy trying not to have a heart attack.

The coaster rocketed through pitch-black corridors, glowing red eyes staring at them from the shadows. Figures moved in the darkness—unreal shapes twisting and reaching—

"DO NOT LOOK BEHIND YOU."

Leighton immediately looked behind her.

A shadowy hand lunged toward her face.

“AAAAAAAAAAAAAAAHHHHHHHHHHHHHH—”

The Nightmare

The ride dropped.

Ulysses screamed.

Leighton screamed louder.

The ground seemed to disappear, leaving them in freefall for what felt like an eternity.

Then, without warning—

"DON’T BLINK."

A wall of faces appeared in the dark, all staring at them with wide, empty eyes.

Ulysses grabbed the harness.

“WHAT KIND OF RIDE IS THIS?!”

Leighton couldn’t even breathe.

Suddenly, the ride stopped.

Total silence.

No wind. No sounds. No movement.

Just… nothing.

Ulysses swallowed.

“…Is it over?”

Leighton didn’t move. She was too scared to even blink.

Then—

"NO."

BAM—

A bloodcurdling scream echoed through the ride as they were launched backward at full speed.

Leighton completely lost her mind.

The jumpscares were endless. Every turn—every loop—every drop—something lurched at them from the darkness.

  • A grotesque figure hanging upside down from the tracks.
  • A distorted laugh echoing from nowhere.
  • A pair of hands reaching out of the shadows.

"THE RIDE NEVER ENDS."

Leighton gasped for air.

Ulysses, for the first time in his life, looked genuinely terrified.

Then—

SILENCE.

The ride pulled back into the station.

The harness released.

Leighton sat there, completely frozen.

Ulysses?

Didn’t move.

The ride operators casually strolled up.

“Welcome back.”

Leighton’s hands were still clenched so tightly around the safety bar that her fingers had locked.

Ulysses blinked slowly, his entire face blank.

Fritz and the others, waiting outside, were howling with laughter.

Leighton turned to Ulysses, her voice deadpan.

“I hate you.”

Ulysses just stared forward.

“...That was not normal.”

The Aftermath

They never spoke of the ride again.
