CHAPTER 3

Group Project Chaos

Leighton liked working alone. She liked having control over her tasks, knowing exactly what needed to be done, and avoiding unnecessary conversations. But school had a cruel way of forcing her into group projects, and this time, she wasn’t just stuck with random classmates—she was stuck with Ulysses.

The moment their teacher announced the groups, she felt a sense of impending doom settle in her stomach. It was a history project. A research paper on historical events, followed by a presentation. Leighton hated presentations. She would rather write a thousand essays than stand in front of the class and speak. And now, she had to do it with Ulysses.

He, of course, looked completely relaxed about it, lazily spinning a pen between his fingers as their teacher finished explaining the assignment.

"Alright, everyone, you’ll have one week to complete this. Make sure you work together," the teacher said before dismissing them.

As soon as class ended, Ulysses turned to her with his usual grin.

"Lucky you," he said. "You get to spend even more time with me."

Leighton sighed deeply, already regretting her life choices.

They met at the school library after class. Leighton had come prepared, bringing a list of possible topics and a notebook for notes. Ulysses, on the other hand, showed up with nothing. He slouched in his chair, arms behind his head, looking at her like he had no plans of doing actual work.

"Okay," she said, sliding the list toward him. "Pick one."

Ulysses lazily scanned the paper before smirking.

"You pick."

Leighton’s eye twitched.

"I already did the work of making the list."
"Exactly," he said. "You clearly know more about this than I do, so I trust your judgment."

Leighton hated how easily he twisted things to his advantage. She exhaled sharply, crossing her arms.

"Fine. The Industrial Revolution."
"Sounds boring," he commented.
"Then you pick."
He grinned. "Nah, I’ll stick with yours. I like watching you get mad."

She wanted to hit him. But instead, she opened her notebook and started jotting down ideas.

For the first ten minutes, Ulysses didn’t contribute anything. He just watched her, resting his chin on his hand. Finally, she turned to him with an unimpressed look.

"Are you going to help?"
He smirked. "I am helping. I’m making sure you don’t mess up."

That was it. She grabbed her notebook and smacked him lightly on the arm.

"Ow," he said, laughing. "Alright, alright, I’ll help."

To her surprise, once he actually tried, he wasn’t completely useless. He had some decent input, and his handwriting wasn’t terrible. They worked in relative peace for a while, with only occasional distractions from Ulysses. But of course, peace never lasted long.

Their quiet study session was interrupted when a familiar voice spoke up.

"Oh, look who it is."

Nathan, Greg, and Oliver. Again.

Leighton tensed immediately, gripping her pen tighter. The three of them strolled up to their table, smirking like usual.

"Doing homework, huh?" Nathan sneered. "That’s so cute. You two having a little study date?"
Greg chuckled. "Bet Leighton does all the work, huh?"
Oliver leaned closer. "You should let Ulysses handle the presentation. Wouldn’t want you freezing up in front of everyone."

Leighton felt her face heat up. She hated how they always knew exactly how to get under her skin. Before she could react, Ulysses sighed dramatically.

"You guys seriously have nothing better to do, huh?" he said. "Kinda sad."
Nathan scoffed. "Relax, man, we’re just messing around."
"Yeah?" Ulysses leaned back in his chair, unimpressed. "Well, here’s a joke for you: three guys walk into a library, and nobody cares."
Greg blinked. "...What?"
"Exactly," Ulysses said. "Nobody cares."

Silence. Oliver actually looked a little offended. Nathan clicked his tongue.

"Whatever," he muttered, shoving his hands in his pockets. "Let’s go."

They walked off, leaving Leighton and Ulysses alone again.

Leighton exhaled, trying to relax. Ulysses glanced at her.

"You okay?"

She hesitated, then gave a small nod.

He grinned. "Good. ‘Cause you still owe me for doing all this research."
She frowned. "You barely did anything."
He smirked. "Exactly. And you let me get away with it. So really, this is your fault."

She grabbed her notebook again. He dodged before she could smack him with it.

They continued working for another hour before deciding to take a break. Ulysses stretched his arms over his head.

"We should do the rest at your place."

Leighton immediately shook her head. Absolutely not.

"Oh, c’mon," he said. "We can’t finish everything here."

She still refused. Ulysses sighed dramatically.

"Fine, fine. My place, then."

She hesitated. That… wasn’t as bad. Reluctantly, she nodded.

Later that evening, she found herself standing outside Ulysses’ house. It was the first time she had been there, and she was surprised by how normal it looked. He opened the door and grinned.

"Welcome to my kingdom."

She rolled her eyes and stepped inside. His house was messy, but not disgusting. There were books and papers scattered on the table, a gaming console near the TV, and a pile of laundry shoved in a corner.

"Make yourself at home," he said, flopping onto the couch.

She took a seat at the table instead, pulling out her notebook.

"Work first," she said firmly.
Ulysses groaned. "You’re such a nerd."

She ignored him and started going through their notes. Eventually, he gave in and joined her. They actually managed to get a lot done, despite Ulysses getting distracted every five minutes.

"Hey, Leighton," he said randomly. "Did you know your handwriting is really tiny?"

She didn’t look up.

"...And neat."

That made her pause. Slowly, she glanced at him. He was looking at her notes, his expression oddly serious.

"It suits you," he added.

For some reason, her face felt warm. She quickly looked away.

"...Shut up."
He smirked. "Did I just make you blush?"
She kicked his leg under the table. "Ow! Okay, okay, I take it back."

She doubted that. But she let it go.

By the time they finished, it was late. Ulysses walked her home, hands in his pockets, whistling casually.

"You know," he said, "you’re kinda fun when you’re not trying to kill me."
Leighton rolled her eyes. "And you’re tolerable when you’re actually useful."
He laughed. "High praise."

They reached her house, and she hesitated before mumbling,

"Thanks… for earlier."

He blinked, surprised. Then, he grinned.

"Anytime, Leighton."

She quickly turned away and went inside, ignoring the way her heart felt weirdly light.
