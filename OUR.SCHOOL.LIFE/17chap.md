[chapter-17]# Chapter 17

[the-breaking-point]## The Breaking Point

The weekend air carried a tense quietness, the kind that usually signaled an upcoming storm. But for Ulysses and his classmates, the only storm they were preparing for was the exam on Monday.

The group had agreed to meet at the library for a full day of studying. Normally, Ulysses would’ve rather done anything else—playing games, wandering the city, teasing Leighton—but this time, even he had to admit that he needed to focus. The last thing he wanted was to fail and have to deal with teachers lecturing him.

When he arrived, most of his classmates were already there. Fritz and Christian were flipping through their science notes, arguing over some complex formulas. Daniel, Leonard, and Val were hunched over history books, while Yeshe was busy copying notes.

And then there was Leighton.

She sat at the far end of the table, her usual hoodie pulled up, flipping through a thick math textbook. Her face was hidden, but Ulysses could tell she was completely focused, her pen scribbling quickly across her notebook.

He walked up and dropped his bag on the table.

> "Studying that hard, huh?"

Leighton didn’t even look up.

Ulysses smirked.

> "You’re gonna burn a hole through that page if you keep writing like that."

This time, she did glance at him—just for a second—before returning to her work.

Fritz snickered.

> "Dude, just admit she’s smarter than you and move on."

Ulysses rolled his eyes and sat down.

> "Whatever. Let’s just get this over with."

The next few hours were filled with the sounds of pages turning, the scratch of pens, and the occasional groans from someone struggling with a difficult problem. Even Ulysses found himself actually trying, which surprised him.

But then, just when the group was settling into a productive rhythm, it happened.

The doors of the library swung open with a loud thud.

A group of students strolled in, their voices echoing through the quiet space.

Ulysses felt his stomach drop.

He didn’t even have to turn around to know who it was.

The bullies.

Led by Royce, the group of older, stronger students walked in like they owned the place. Royce himself was tall, muscular, and had that smug look on his face—the one that made Ulysses want to punch him. But he knew better. Royce and his gang weren’t just talk. They were dangerous.

Royce’s gaze swept over the library before landing on them.

A slow smirk spread across his face.

Ulysses clenched his jaw. Not today.

But Royce had already started walking toward them, his friends following close behind.

> "Well, well," Royce sneered, stopping beside their table." What do we have here? A little study group?"

His gang chuckled, surrounding the table.

Leonard frowned.

> "Just leave us alone, man."

Royce ignored him. His eyes landed on Leighton.

She had gone completely still.

Royce smirked.

> "Hey, look. It’s the little quiet girl."

Leighton didn’t respond.

One of the other bullies leaned in closer.

> "What’s wrong? Too scared to talk?"

Royce chuckled.

> "Maybe she thinks she’s better than us."

Ulysses could see it—the way Leighton’s hands trembled slightly.

Royce suddenly reached out and snatched Leighton’s notebook off the table.

Leighton jolted up, her face paling.

> "Give it back," she muttered, barely audible.
> "What was that?" Royce taunted, waving the notebook around." Speak up."

Leighton bit her lip, but she didn’t back down.

> "I said give it back."

Royce smirked.

> "Or what?"

Ulysses tensed. He wanted to step in, but he knew how this worked. Royce and his gang were too strong. Fighting back would only make things worse.

Leighton stood up abruptly, reaching for her notebook, but Royce yanked it away, laughing.

> "Hey!" Fritz snapped." Just give it back!"

But Royce wasn’t done yet. He flipped through the pages, then suddenly ripped one out.

A sharp rip echoed in the library.

Leighton’s breath hitched.

Ulysses saw it—the way her eyes widened, the way her hands clenched into fists.

Royce crumpled the page and tossed it onto the floor.

> "Oops."

Silence.

Then, without warning, Leighton lunged at him.

Royce barely had time to react before she grabbed his wrist, trying to snatch the notebook back. But he was stronger. With one swift movement, he shoved her backward.

Leighton stumbled, knocking over her chair.

The entire library went silent.

Ulysses stood up immediately.

> "That’s enough."

Royce raised an eyebrow.

> "Oh? You wanna do something about it?"

Ulysses knew he couldn’t win, but seeing Leighton on the ground, humiliated, made his blood boil.

Before he could move, Royce’s friends stepped forward, cracking their knuckles.

> "You should stay down," one of them sneered.

Ulysses hesitated. They were too strong.

Royce smirked.

> "That’s what I thought."

He turned back to Leighton, who was slowly picking herself up.

> "You should just stay quiet like always," he said, tossing her notebook onto the floor." You’re pathetic."

Leighton’s face burned red—not just from embarrassment, but from pure rage. But she didn’t say anything.

The bullies laughed as they walked away, leaving Ulysses and his classmates frozen in place.

Fritz clenched his fists.

> "We should’ve done something."

Val nodded, furious.

> "I hate them."

Leighton, however, said nothing. She simply picked up her notebook, dusted it off, and sat back down, her face unreadable.

Ulysses watched her, feeling a strange tightness in his chest.

He had never seen her look so small before.

And for the first time, he hated how powerless he was.
