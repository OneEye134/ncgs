[chapter-19-the-game-of-endurance]# Chapter 19: The Game of Endurance

The schoolyard buzzed with anticipation as students gathered for P. E. class. Today wasn’t just any game of tag—it was a battle. On one side stood Ulysses, Leighton, Leonard, Christian, Val, Yeshe, Daniel, and Fritz. On the other, Royce and his gang. The rivalry between the two groups was no secret, and everyone knew this was going to get competitive fast.

Ulysses cracked his knuckles and stretched his arms.

> **"Alright, same plan as always. We work together and don’t get caught."**

Leighton stood silently beside him, her arms crossed. She already regretted being here. She hated sports, hated running, and definitely hated the idea of being chased by Royce and his friends. But the teams were set, and backing out wasn’t an option.

The teacher blew the whistle.

Chaos.

Royce’s team surged forward like hunters on the prowl. Ulysses’ group scattered, ducking between trees, benches, and clusters of students. Leonard and Christian sprinted off first, drawing some of the bullies with them. Daniel and Fritz stuck close together, weaving unpredictably between obstacles. Val and Yeshe held their ground near the center, watching for chances to strike back.

Leighton was already struggling. Her legs felt heavy, and each step drained her strength. She wasn’t fast, and she knew it.

It wasn’t long before Royce locked eyes on her, grinning as he started to chase.

> **"Why do you look so scared?"**
> Royce taunted, his strides closing the distance with ease.

Leighton pushed herself to move faster, but her body was already tiring. Just when it seemed like Royce would catch her, Ulysses appeared, grabbing her wrist and pulling her away.

> **"Move it, slowpoke,"**
> Ulysses said with a smirk, dragging her behind him.

Leighton shot him an annoyed glare but didn’t protest. She focused on keeping pace, her heart pounding as they dodged between classmates.

Meanwhile, Leonard had led a bully straight into a tree, and Christian danced around another with sharp footwork. Daniel and Fritz trapped one of Royce’s friends between two benches, forcing him to waste precious time figuring out how to escape.

Royce wasn’t giving up. His team pushed harder, their frustration growing as their targets slipped away. Every time someone slowed, a bully closed in, forcing them to keep moving.

The game stretched longer than anyone expected. It became a battle of endurance, not just speed. Everyone was panting, but none of them wanted to lose. Even Leighton, who hated every second of this, refused to be the first one caught.

At one point, Ulysses and Royce crossed paths. They locked eyes, their rivalry sharp and unspoken.

> **"You look tired,"**
> Ulysses teased.

Royce lunged, but Ulysses sidestepped easily, letting Royce stumble forward. The frustration on Royce’s face was almost worth the exhaustion.

Finally, the teacher blew the whistle, signaling the end of the game. Almost everyone collapsed, gasping for air.

Leighton sat on the grass, glaring at Ulysses, who grinned down at her.

> **"You owe me for saving you."**

Leighton rolled her eyes and looked away, too exhausted to argue.

Royce and his friends muttered complaints, annoyed they hadn’t dominated the game like they wanted. But Ulysses’ team, despite their fatigue, knew they had held their own.

As the group slowly recovered, Ulysses stretched his arms and glanced at Leighton.

> **"Guess you don’t hate P. E. that much now, huh?"**
> he teased.

Leighton grabbed a nearby water bottle and tossed it at him.
