[chapter-1]# CHAPTER 1

Leighton was used to avoiding people. It was something she had perfected over the years, the ability to slip through crowds unnoticed, to make herself as small as possible, to ensure that no one paid her any mind. It wasn’t that she disliked people—she just didn’t know how to interact with them. Words got stuck in her throat too easily, and her brain always overanalyzed every little thing.

That was why Ulysses was such a problem. He didn’t let her disappear. No matter how much she tried to ignore him, he always found a way to insert himself into her day, whether it was through teasing, annoying questions, or simply existing too close to her personal space.

And today, of course, was no different.

As soon as she stepped outside her house, there he was, leaning against the gate like he owned the place. His hands were in his pockets, his posture relaxed, and that infuriating smirk was already in place.

> “Morning, Leighton,” he greeted casually. “Have any good dreams about me?”

She froze mid-step, her fingers tightening around the strap of her bag. Slowly, she turned her head, giving him a glare that could’ve set things on fire.

He chuckled, clearly enjoying her reaction.

> “Wow, you didn’t even deny it. That means yes, right?”

Leighton let out a sharp breath, her cheeks already turning red. She wanted to argue, to tell him he was being ridiculous, but she knew how these conversations went. If she engaged, it would only encourage him more.

So instead, she turned on her heel and started walking, pretending he didn’t exist.

Unfortunately, pretending Ulysses didn’t exist never worked. He was persistent, effortlessly keeping up with her pace, hands still shoved in his pockets like he had all the time in the world.

> “So, what was I doing in your dream?” he continued, his tone dripping with amusement. “Was I super cool? Maybe saving you from some bad guys? Oh, wait, was it a romantic dream? Were we—”

Leighton stopped so suddenly that he almost walked into her. Then, without a word, she turned slightly and stomped on his foot. Hard.

> “Owww—okay, okay, I deserved that,” Ulysses laughed, hopping on one leg.

Leighton didn’t even glance at him before resuming her walk. She could still hear his laughter behind her, though, and that annoyed her more than anything.

The walk to school was mostly peaceful after that, with Ulysses occasionally making comments that she ignored. But when they reached the school gates, she immediately tensed.

A group of students stood near the entrance, talking loudly, their presence impossible to ignore. And among them were three people Leighton would have rather avoided: Nathan, Greg, and Oliver. The school bullies.

They weren’t the kind of bullies who physically hurt people, but they thrived on making others uncomfortable, pushing buttons until their victims squirmed. And for some reason, they loved picking on Leighton. Maybe it was because she never fought back. Maybe because they thought it was funny how easily flustered she got.

Whatever the reason, their attention always seemed to land on her.

And, as expected, the moment they saw her, they smirked.

> “Well, well, if it isn’t the quiet little mouse,” Nathan drawled, stepping forward with an easy grin. “You finally decided to talk yet?”

Greg snickered.

> “Doubt it. Bet she still squeaks when she tries.”

Leighton stiffened, gripping her bag tightly. She wanted to walk past them, to pretend she hadn’t heard anything, but she knew they wouldn’t let her off that easily.

Before they could say anything else, however, Ulysses casually stepped in front of her, blocking her from view. His smirk was gone, replaced with an unreadable expression.

> “Oh, hey, I didn’t know losers had a meeting this early in the morning,” he said, tilting his head. “Is there, like, a sign-up sheet for that?”

Nathan’s grin faltered slightly.

> “What’s your problem?”

Ulysses shrugged.

> “No problem. Just wondering why three guys are wasting their time bothering someone who clearly doesn’t care about them.”

He leaned forward slightly, lowering his voice just enough to be threatening.

> “Or are you guys that desperate for attention?”

Greg scoffed.

> “Whatever, man. We were just messing around.”
> “Yeah,” Oliver added. “No need to get all defensive.”

Ulysses smiled, but there was no humor in it.

> “Oh, I’m not defensive. I just think it’s pathetic.”

The three bullies exchanged glances before deciding it wasn’t worth the trouble. With a few muttered insults under their breath, they turned and walked away.

Leighton let out a breath she hadn’t realized she was holding.

Ulysses turned back to her, his usual smirk returning.

> “You okay?”

She hesitated for a moment, then gave a small nod.

> “Good,” he said. “But, you know, if you ever wanna punch one of them, I’ll hold them still for you.”

Her lips twitched slightly, almost like she wanted to smile, but she quickly looked away.

Ulysses didn’t say anything else as they walked inside. The rest of the day was uneventful, aside from the usual teasing from Ulysses. He stole her eraser during class, poked her arm just to see her reaction, and made dumb jokes at lunch while she silently tolerated him.

But despite all of that, she didn’t really mind his presence. He was annoying, yes, but he never crossed a line. And, sometimes, like this morning, he actually made her feel… safe.

The realization made her blush, and she quickly shook the thought away.

After school, they walked home together again, like they always did. The sun was beginning to set, casting long shadows across the pavement. The air was warm, the sound of distant chatter and barking dogs filling the silence between them.

Ulysses, for once, wasn’t teasing her. He was just walking beside her, quiet, as if he was content just being there.

It was strange. Leighton had spent most of her life avoiding people, yet somehow, Ulysses had wedged himself into her daily routine without her realizing it. And, even stranger, she wasn’t sure she minded.

As they reached her house, she paused at the gate, hesitating for a brief second before looking at him. She opened her mouth slightly, as if trying to say something, but no words came out.

Ulysses noticed and raised an eyebrow.

> “What? You finally gonna confess your love for me?”

Leighton’s face immediately turned red, and before he could say anything else, she lightly kicked his shin.

> “Ow! Okay, okay, I deserved that,” he laughed, rubbing his leg.

She huffed, turned away, and disappeared inside her house.

Ulysses watched her go, a small grin on his lips.

He wasn’t sure when it happened, but at some point, teasing Leighton had become his favorite part of life.
