[chapter-4]# Chapter 4

[a-market-mishap]## A Market Mishap

Leighton didn't like crowded places. The noise, the people, the overwhelming energy—it was all too much for her. Unfortunately, she had no choice but to go to the public market today. Her mother had given her a list of ingredients to buy, and refusing wasn’t an option.

She had planned to go early in the morning, hoping to avoid the crowd, but somehow, Ulysses found out.

> “You’re going to the market?” he asked, grinning. “Great! I’ll come with you.”

She immediately regretted mentioning it. She tried to shake him off, walking ahead, but he effortlessly kept up.

> “You should be grateful, you know,” he teased. “The market is full of scammers. What if someone tricks you into buying fake vegetables?”
> She shot him a glare. "That doesn't even make sense."
> He smirked. “Exactly. And you’d probably fall for it.”

She huffed and kept walking.

---

The market was a chaotic mix of sounds and smells. Vendors shouted out prices, people weaved through the narrow paths, and the scent of fresh produce mixed with the sharp aroma of fish. Leighton hated it. Ulysses, on the other hand, looked completely relaxed, hands in his pockets as he strolled beside her.

> “What’s first on the list?” he asked.

She ignored him and went straight to the vegetable stall. As she picked out some onions, the vendor, an older woman with a sharp gaze, smiled.

> “Oh, shopping with your boyfriend?”

Leighton froze. Ulysses, of course, took full advantage of the situation.

> “Yup,” he said smoothly. “She dragged me here against my will, though.”

Leighton turned red.

> “H-He’s not my—”
> “She’s shy,” Ulysses interrupted. “But yeah, she’s totally in love with me.”

The vendor laughed.

> “Ah, young love.”

Leighton wanted to disappear. She quickly paid for the vegetables and stormed off, ignoring Ulysses’ laughter behind her.

> “Hey, wait up!” he called, catching up easily. “You’re so easy to mess with.”

She shot him a glare.

> "Shut up."
> “Nope,” he said cheerfully. “Not until you admit you love dragging me around.”

She clenched her fists, resisting the urge to throw a tomato at him. They still had more shopping to do.

---

At the fruit stall, Ulysses immediately started causing trouble.

> “Hey, Leighton,” he said, picking up a mango. “What if I just—”

Before she could stop him, he threw the mango in the air. It landed with a loud thud, rolling off the table. The vendor, a grumpy old man, turned to glare at them.

> “You break it, you buy it.”

Ulysses, unfazed, picked up the mango and handed it to Leighton.

> “Here. Your fault.”

Her eye twitched.

> “I didn't even touch it.”
> “Exactly,” he said. “You should’ve stopped me.”

She sighed, handing the vendor some money.

> “You’re such a responsible girlfriend.”

She nearly choked.

> “I—”
> “Shh,” he said. “It’s okay. You don’t have to deny it.”

She kicked his shin.

---

As they continued shopping, the crowd got thicker. Leighton hated it. The noise, the pushing—it made her anxious. Ulysses noticed.

> “Stick close,” he said, his tone suddenly serious. “Or you’ll get lost.”

She didn’t want to. But when someone accidentally bumped into her, she instinctively grabbed onto the sleeve of Ulysses’ jacket. He glanced down at her, surprised. She quickly let go, looking away.

> "Forget that happened."

He smirked.

> “Nah. I think I’ll remember it forever.”

---

The last stop was the meat section. The strong smell made Leighton feel slightly nauseous, but she focused on finishing the task. Ulysses, however, was distracted.

> “Hey,” he said, nudging her. “That guy looks shady.”

She followed his gaze. A man in a hoodie was lingering near one of the stalls, glancing around suspiciously.

> “What if he’s a thief?” Ulysses whispered. “Should we do something?”
> “You should mind your own business,” she muttered.
> “What if he steals something?”

Before she could stop him, Ulysses marched up to the man.

> “Hey,” he said casually. “You look like you’re about to commit a crime.”

The man stiffened. Leighton’s soul left her body.

> “U-Ulysses, stop,” she whispered urgently.

The man gave them a weird look before walking away. Ulysses smirked.

> “See? Suspicious.”

Leighton sighed, regretting everything.

---

After they finally finished shopping, they headed home. Leighton was exhausted.

> “See?” Ulysses said as they walked. “Aren’t you glad I came?”

She glared at him.

> "No."

He laughed.

> “Liar.”

She sighed, deciding she was too tired to argue.

---

As they reached her house, Ulysses suddenly leaned in closer.

> "By the way," he whispered. "You still owe me for the mango."

Leighton slammed the gate in his face.
