CHAPTER 14

How Not to Shop with "Friends"

Leighton had one rule when it came to outings: avoid public places at all costs.

Unfortunately, that rule was impossible to follow when she was dragged into the middle of a crowded market by Ulysses, Daniel , and Fritz, who had somehow convinced her that this was necessary for their project.

“We need materials,” Ulysses had said, smirking.
“And besides, wouldn’t it be fun to go shopping together?”

Leighton had immediately refused, but before she could escape, Daniel had grabbed her by the wrist, Fritz had blocked the door, and Ulysses had given her his most infuriating grin.

And now here she was—standing in the middle of a noisy marketplace, surrounded by chaos, her arms tightly crossed as she glared at her so-called teammates.

“Alright,” she said, voice flat, “we get what we need and leave. Fast.”

“Aww, come on, Leighton,” Muhammad laughed, slinging an arm around her shoulders. “Relax a little. Markets are fun.”

“Not when you three are involved.”

“Ouch,” Fritz smirked, shoving his hands in his pockets. “That hurts, Leighton. That really hurts.”

“It doesn’t.”

Ulysses chuckled, nudging her lightly.

“Alright, alright. Let’s make this quick before Leighton actually murders us.”

Leighton sighed in relief, but that relief was short-lived.

Because the second they entered the first stall, everything went downhill.

Fritz had somehow knocked over an entire stack of fruit baskets, sending oranges rolling everywhere while the stall owner yelled in frustration.

Daniel, meanwhile, was haggling aggressively, even though they didn’t actually need to buy anything from that particular vendor.

And Ulysses?
He was watching the chaos unfold with a smirk, occasionally throwing out unhelpful comments like:

“Wow, this is going well.”
“Hey Leighton, do you think they’ll ban us from the market?”

Leighton rubbed her temples, feeling a headache brewing.

“I hate all of you,” she muttered.

“No, you don’t,” Ulysses grinned.

“Yes, I do.”

“Then why are you still here?”

She had no answer. Instead, she grabbed Ulysses by the sleeve and dragged him away from the mess, ignoring his laughter as she marched toward the next stall.

“If we don’t get everything in the next ten minutes, I’m leaving all of you behind.”

“So scary" Fritz muttered, still picking up the fruit he had knocked over.

“I heard that.”

“Of course you did.”

The next stall was less chaotic, but that was only because Leighton took full control, grabbing the items they needed and shoving them into Ulysses’ arms.

“Carry this,” she ordered.

“Wow, bossy,” he mused. “Are you always this scary when you shop?”

“Yes. Move.”

They continued through the market, but the peace didn’t last long.

At one point, Daniel challenged a random kid to a spicy food eating contest.

Fritz got distracted by a street magician and almost got pickpocketed.

And Ulysses?
He kept making Leighton carry things, just to see how long it would take before she snapped.

(Spoiler: Not long. She threw a bag of vegetables at his face.)

By the time they finally reached the last stall, Leighton was exhausted.

“There,” she muttered, checking the list. “We’re done.”

“Aww, already?” Daniel sighed. “I was having fun.”

“You caused at least three near-disasters!”

“And?”

Leighton ignored him. Instead, she turned to Ulysses, who had been oddly quiet for the past few minutes.

“What are you doing?” she asked, suspicious.

“Oh, nothing,” he said casually, but there was something in his tone that immediately put her on edge.

“What did you do.”

“Nothing.”

She narrowed her eyes.

“Ulysses—”

“Hey, Leighton, quick question,” he interrupted, smirking. “How do you feel about running?”

“What—”

“Because we should probably run. Right now.”

Before she could process that, a very angry vendor stormed toward them, yelling something about not touching things they weren’t planning to buy.

Leighton froze.

“Ulysses,” she hissed, already backing away. “What did you do?”

“No time for questions—RUN!”

And just like that, the four of them bolted, weaving through the crowded market as the vendor chased after them, yelling threats that Leighton really didn’t want to translate.
