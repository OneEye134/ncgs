Chapter 18: Unwanted Competition

The midday sun cast a golden glare over the school's outdoor sports field. It was another dreaded PE class—dreaded, at least, by Leighton. While the others stretched and chatted, preparing for the activity ahead, she sat quietly on the farthest edge of the bleachers, knees hugged to her chest.

Meanwhile, Ulysses stood on the field with his usual group—Leonard, Christian, Val, Yeshe, Daniel, and Fritz—each one radiating restless energy.

"Man, I hope we get some real competition today," Ulysses muttered, cracking his knuckles.

"Yeah, but not against someone completely useless," Christian added with a grin.

"Like Leighton," Daniel said, laughing.

Their words floated across the field. Leighton heard them, but she didn’t react. She was used to it by now. Used to being the afterthought, the weak link, the joke. She just wanted the class to end.

The sharp blow of the PE teacher’s whistle broke through the chatter.

"Alright, listen up! Today’s game is dodgeball! We’ll divide into two teams. You'll be playing against the other section!"

Groans and excited cheers erupted from the students. The other section had a reputation—aggressive, competitive, ruthless.

Ulysses’ team stuck together. No surprise there. Leonard, Christian, Val, Yeshe, Daniel, Fritz, and Ulysses—ready to dominate. Leighton, however, was randomly assigned to the opposing team, alongside the other section.

When she stepped onto the court, she felt every pair of eyes on her. Judging. Waiting.

Ulysses gave her a mocking glance.

"Try not to get hit in the face, Leighton."

She didn’t answer, just tightened her jaw.

The whistle blew.

Chaos erupted.

Balls soared across the court. Ulysses and his team moved like a machine—dodging, catching, and throwing with deadly precision. Christian took out two players in one throw. Fritz ducked just in time to avoid a fastball. Daniel laughed as he tagged someone with a sharp shot to the legs.

And Leighton? She flinched with every ball that whizzed past her. She wasn’t fast enough. She wasn’t strong enough. She wasn’t enough.

A blur of red flew at her chest. She hesitated. Too slow.

The impact knocked the breath out of her. She stumbled, pain blooming across her ribs.

"OUT!" the referee called.

Snickers broke out from Ulysses’ team.

"That was fast," Val chuckled.

Leighton didn’t look at them. She walked off the court, head down, throat tight. She sat back on the bleachers, wishing she could disappear.

The game ended quickly. Ulysses’ team won, as expected.

As they gathered to cool down, Ulysses strolled past Leighton, ruffling her hair.

"You were totally useless, as expected."

Leighton slapped his hand away, face burning.

"Shut up."

Ulysses only grinned.

"You should at least try next time."

Before she could respond, a sharp voice sliced through the air.

"Well, well, look who we have here."

Royce.

He led his group of bullies across the field, that same smug grin on his face. His lackeys followed, eyes gleaming with mischief.

Leighton’s pulse quickened.

"Heard you got knocked out of the game in seconds," Royce said, his tone dripping with mockery. "Guess that’s expected from someone like you."

Ulysses stepped forward, his grin fading.

"Get lost, Royce."

Royce chuckled.

"Why? Just having a little fun."

Without warning, Royce grabbed Leighton’s wrist and yanked her to her feet.

"Let go," she hissed, struggling.

"Make me," Royce taunted, his grip tightening.

Ulysses’ fists clenched. The last time he fought Royce, he’d lost. Badly.

"Let her go, man," Christian warned, stepping beside Ulysses.

But Royce only laughed.

"Relax, we’re just playing."

His grip tightened until Leighton winced.

Ulysses moved before he could think. He grabbed Royce’s arm, yanking it away.

"I said, let go."

For a split second, Royce looked surprised. Then, his grin widened.

"Oh? You wanna go again?"

Before Ulysses could react, Royce’s fist slammed into his stomach.

The air left his lungs as he staggered back, gasping.

"Ulysses!" Leighton cried.

Royce’s lackeys laughed.

"Still think you can take him?" one jeered.

Ulysses clenched his teeth, clutching his stomach.

Leighton’s breath shook. Her hands balled into fists.

"Stop," she muttered.

Royce turned, amused.

"What was that?"

"I said stop!" she snapped.

Silence.

Then Royce laughed, releasing her wrist.

"Feisty. Fine. I’m done here anyway."

He shoved Ulysses one last time before turning away.

"See you losers later."

The bullies walked off, their laughter fading into the distance.

Ulysses groaned, rubbing his stomach.

"Man, I really hate that guy."

Leighton said nothing, her hand still trembling slightly.

Ulysses glanced at her.

"You okay?"

She looked away.

"I’m fine."

He smirked weakly.

"You actually yelled at him. That’s new."

Leighton shot him a glare, though there was no heat behind it.

"Shut up."

Ulysses’ grin widened.

"Sure."
