[chapter-5]# Chapter 5

[a-rainy-day-and-a-project-disaster]## Stormy Skies and Unlikely Ties

Leighton hated group projects. It wasn’t because she disliked working with others—she just wasn’t good at talking. Whenever teachers assigned group work, she would freeze, unable to speak up, which usually led to her doing all the work alone.

That’s exactly why today was going to be a nightmare. Their history teacher, Mr. Navarro, had assigned a research project, and of all people, Leighton was stuck with Ulysses.

She groaned internally as she stared at the seating chart on the board.

Ulysses, of course, was thrilled.

> “Looks like we’re partners, Leighton,” he said, leaning on her desk with his usual grin. “Excited?”

She turned her head away, hoping he would take the hint and disappear. He didn’t.

> “So, your place or mine?” he asked casually.

She flinched, her face turning red.

> “... The library.”
> “Boring,” he sighed. “Fine, but we’re getting snacks after.”

She refused to respond.

---

By the time school ended, the sky had darkened. Thunder rumbled in the distance, and thick clouds promised a heavy downpour. Leighton frowned. She had forgotten her umbrella.

Ulysses, noticing her hesitation, smirked.

> “You scared of a little rain?”

She shook her head and walked faster toward the library, hoping to avoid talking to him. It didn’t work.

> “What’s the project about again?” Ulysses asked as he followed.
> “You weren’t listening?” she muttered.
> “Not really,” he admitted. “I figured you’d remember for me.”

She wanted to strangle him.

---

Inside the library, they found a table in the corner. Leighton pulled out her notebook, jotting down ideas while Ulysses rested his chin on his palm, clearly uninterested.

> “We should divide the work,” she said quietly. “You do half, I do half.”

Ulysses nodded lazily.

> “Sounds fair. You do the research, writing, and presentation. I’ll provide moral support.”

She stabbed his foot under the table.

> “Ow—okay, okay, I’ll help,” he chuckled.

She sighed, handing him a book.

> “Start with this.”

He flipped through the pages absentmindedly.

> “Leighton, do you ever have fun?”

She didn’t respond. She didn’t need to.

---

An hour passed, and Ulysses had done absolutely nothing. Leighton was about to scold him when a loud clap of thunder shook the library. She flinched.

Ulysses noticed and smirked.

> “Oh? Are you scared?”

She refused to look at him.

> “No.”

Another thunderclap. She visibly tensed.

Ulysses chuckled.

> “Liar.”

She shot him a glare but quickly turned back to her book, pretending he didn’t exist.

He wasn’t fooled.

> “Do you want me to protect you?” he teased.

She considered throwing the book at his face.

---

Just as she was about to respond, the rain poured down. Heavy drops pounded against the library windows, and students groaned, realizing they would be stuck inside for a while.

Leighton sighed. This was not how she planned her day.

Ulysses, however, looked amused.

> “Guess we’re stuck here,” he said, stretching. “Might as well take a break.”
> “We haven’t even started,” she muttered.
> “Exactly. No need to rush.”

She clenched her fists.

---

After another half hour of barely any progress, Ulysses leaned back.

> “You know, this isn’t so bad. I get to watch you work while I relax.”
> “Do something,” she snapped.
> “Fine, fine.”

He picked up a pen and started doodling in the margins of their notes. Leighton wanted to scream.

---

Eventually, the rain lightened up. Students slowly left the library, but Leighton hesitated. Ulysses noticed.

> “Still no umbrella?”

She shook her head.

He grinned, pulling something out of his bag—a bright yellow umbrella.

> “Lucky for you, I came prepared.”

She eyed it warily.

> “... I’ll wait until it stops.”
> “We’ll be here all night,” he said. “Come on, unless you want to be trapped with me forever.”

She shuddered at the thought.

---

With no other choice, she followed him outside. The rain had slowed, but the wind was still strong, making it hard to walk without getting wet. Ulysses held the umbrella over both of them, but his height made it difficult for Leighton to stay covered.

> “Tilt it lower,” she muttered.
> “What?”
> “Lower.”
> “Huh? Can’t hear you.”

She stomped on his foot.

> “Ow—okay, okay, I’ll lower it!”

She huffed.

---

As they walked through the quiet neighborhood, the rain picked up again. Ulysses sighed.

> “Great. Now we’re both soaked.”

Leighton glanced at him. His hair was damp, and raindrops dripped from his jacket. She hesitated before mumbling,

> “... Your fault.”

He smirked.

> “Everything’s my fault, huh?”

She nodded.

> “Yup.”

He laughed.

---

When they finally reached her house, Leighton turned to go inside. Before she could escape, Ulysses grabbed her sleeve. She froze.

> “Hey,” he said.

She waited, heart pounding. What now?

He smirked.

> “Next time, let’s just do the project at my place. Less chance of drowning.”

She ripped her sleeve out of his grip and slammed the door shut.

---

Through the window, she could still hear his laughter.
