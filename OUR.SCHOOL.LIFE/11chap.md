[chapter-11-the-group-project-disaster]## Chapter 11: The Group Project Disaster

It was supposed to be a simple project—just a poster presentation for their literature class, nothing complicated, nothing stressful, just an easy task that required minimal effort, or at least that’s what Ulysses had thought until he realized that their teacher had decided to randomly assign groups, which, of course, meant that he was now stuck with Leighton, two classmates who never did anything, and one guy who took everything way too seriously.

The moment their group was announced, Ulysses turned to Leighton, who was already visibly tense, her fingers tightening around the strap of her bag as if she could physically brace herself for the impending disaster. He smirked, leaning in slightly.

> “Excited?” he teased.

She shot him a glare that could have burned a hole through steel.

> “No.”“Well, that makes two of us.”

Their other teammates weren’t exactly promising—Leonard and Christian, who were notorious for slacking off, and Kyzen, who had already pulled out a detailed list of things that needed to be done, complete with color-coded tabs and a five-step plan for success.

Ulysses sighed, already feeling the headache forming.

> “Alright, so what’s the plan?”

Kyzen adjusted his glasses, flipping open his notebook.

> “I have everything organized. First, we divide the research into sections. Leighton, you handle the historical context. Ulysses, you take the analysis. Leonard and Christian will—”

He paused, glancing at the two boys, who were already half-asleep in their chairs.

> “…Well, they’ll do something.”

Ulysses snorted.

> “Right. Sounds great.”

Leighton, on the other hand, looked like she wanted to sink into the floor. She hated speaking up, hated group work, hated the pressure of having to interact with people she wasn’t comfortable with, and Ulysses could tell just by the way she kept shifting uncomfortably in her seat that this was already too much for her. Still, she nodded, quietly writing down her assigned task without complaint.

[the-disaster-unfolds]### The Disaster Unfolds

The project itself was a disaster from the start. Leonard and Christian contributed absolutely nothing, Kyzen tried to micromanage every single detail, and Ulysses, well, he was just trying to keep Leighton from combusting because at this point, she looked seconds away from a stress-induced breakdown.

At one point, Kyzen started arguing with Leonard about formatting, Christian was throwing crumpled paper at Ulysses, and Leighton, who had been silent the whole time, suddenly stood up, walked into the kitchen, and came back with a bag of chips, which she opened and started eating as if she had given up on life.

Ulysses raised an eyebrow.

> “That bad, huh?”

She didn’t respond, just kept munching aggressively.

Eventually, Kyzen managed to wrangle everyone back into focus, and somehow, miraculously, they finished the research portion of the project.

By then, it was already dark outside, and Leonard and Christian were snoring on the couch, leaving just Ulysses, , and Leighton to handle the final organization.

> “We need to type everything up,” Kyzen announced, pushing up his glasses. “Leighton, do you have a computer?”

She hesitated before nodding, standing up and leading them to her room, which was, unsurprisingly, small, neat, and filled with books.

[the-walk-home]### The Walk Home

They walked in silence, the streets mostly empty except for the occasional passing car. The air was cool, and the distant sound of crickets filled the space between them.

Ulysses shoved his hands into his pockets, glancing at Leighton out of the corner of his eye. She looked tired but relaxed, her usual tension finally easing.

He smirked.

> “You know,” he said casually, “you were kinda cute back there, stuffing your face with chips while the world burned.”

She stiffened immediately.

> “…Shut up.”

He laughed, tilting his head back to look at the sky.

> “You’re fun, Leighton.”

She didn’t respond, but for once, she didn’t look too annoyed, either.

Maybe, just maybe, this project wasn’t so bad after all.
