Chapter 15

The Restaurant Disaster

Leighton had learned two things about life. First, never trust when he smiles like that . Second, never go anywhere with Ulysses, Marcus, and Jace if she valued her sanity.

Yet here she was.

Sitting in a restaurant booth, arms crossed, regretting every single decision that had led to this moment.

“Cheer up, Leighton,” Ulysses said, nudging her elbow. “It’s just dinner.”

“It’s never just dinner with you people.”

“Ouch,” Fritz smirked, flipping through the menu. “You wound me, Leighton. Deeply.”

“She’s just mad she got dragged into this,” Daniel laughed, stretching his arms. “But come on, isn’t it nice? Just the four of us, chilling, eating good food—”

“Causing public disturbances,” Leighton added flatly.

“Oh, come on.”

She sighed, picking up the menu. The restaurant was small and cozy, the kind of place with big portions and small prices. The air smelled of sizzling meat and spices, and the low hum of conversation filled the space. It was... nice.

Too nice.

Something’s going to go wrong.

She glanced at Ulysses. He was too relaxed. That meant he was planning something.

“What are you up to?” she asked suspiciously.

“Me? Nothing.” His grin said otherwise.

Before she could press further, the waiter arrived.

Daniel, of course, ordered enough food to feed a small army. Fritz kept it simple, Ulysses chose something suspiciously expensive, and Leighton—still wary—picked the safest option on the menu.

For a while, it was... normal. They talked, argued, and ignored Daniel when he started rambling about his “legendary metabolism.”

And for a brief, shining moment, Leighton let herself believe that maybe, just maybe, this wouldn’t be a disaster.

Then the food arrived.

Everything was fine. For exactly two minutes.

Then Daniel, being Daniel, challenged to a spicy food contest.

Fritz, being Fritz, agreed immediately.

And Ulysses, being Ulysses, made it worse.

“You know,” he said, stirring his drink, “Leighton could probably beat both of you.”

Leighton froze.

“No.”

“Aw, come on,” Daniel grinned. “Don’t be scared.”

“I’m not scared. I’m sane.”

“Sounds like something a scared person would say.”

“Coward,” Fritz added with a smirk.

Leighton shot Ulysses a death glare.

“I hate you,” she muttered.

“I know,” he said cheerfully.

And that was how Leighton ended up in a three-way spicy food contest she never wanted to be part of.

At first, it wasn’t so bad. Then the heat hit.

Daniel turned red in the face almost immediately.
Fritz tried to look cool but was clearly suffering.
Leighton? She refused to lose.

Ulysses just sat there, sipping his drink, watching them burn.

“How’s it going?” he asked innocently.

No one answered. They were too busy dying.

Daniel coughed. Fritz's eye twitched. Leighton felt her soul leave her body.

And then—disaster.

Daniel reached for his water, but in his panic, knocked over the entire pitcher.

Ice water spilled across the table, soaking Leighton’s sleeves, splashing onto Fritz’s lap, and sending everyone into chaos.

Fritz shot up so fast his chair nearly toppled. Daniel yelped. Leighton just sat there, drenched, her expression blank.

And Ulysses?

He laughed.

That was his mistake.

Leighton calmly reached for a handful of ice and shoved it down his shirt.

Ulysses let out a strangled yelp.

“COLD—Leighton! WHAT—”

“Oh, I’m sorry,” she said sweetly. “Did I ruin your peaceful meal?”

He shivered, trying to shake out the ice.

“I hate you.”

“I know,” she said, stealing one of his fries.

By the time they left the restaurant, they were soaked, exhausted, and slightly traumatized.

And despite everything, Leighton had to admit—

It was kind of fun.
