CHAPTER 10

Market Stalls and Raindrops

The rain started unexpectedly, coming down in heavy sheets that soaked everything within minutes, and of course, Ulysses and Leighton were caught right in the middle of it.

They had gone to the market after school to grab some supplies for a group project, but now, instead of walking home, they were standing under the narrow awning of a small shop, watching the rain pour.

Ulysses ran a hand through his already-drenched hair, shaking his head.

"Well, this sucks," he muttered, glancing at Leighton, who stood beside him, hugging her bag tightly against her chest.

She was completely still, staring at the rain with an unreadable expression.

"You okay?" he asked, nudging her lightly.

She blinked, as if pulled from deep thought, then nodded once.

Ulysses didn’t quite believe her, but he let it go. Instead, he sighed dramatically.

"Guess we’re stuck here for a while. Hope you like spending time with me."

"Ouch. That actually hurt a little."

She rolled her eyes and pulled her hood up, as if that would make a difference when she was already soaked.

He glanced around, taking in the small market stalls that were still open despite the rain. Vendors sat under umbrellas, watching the storm with tired eyes, while customers hurried past with plastic bags held over their heads. A few kids were laughing, splashing through puddles, completely unbothered by the weather.

Ulysses turned back to Leighton.

"You ever play in the rain as a kid?"

She hesitated, then slowly shook her head.

"Seriously? Never?"

Another shake.

"Man, you really missed out."
"I don’t like getting wet."
"Too late for that," he pointed out.

She huffed but didn’t argue. The silence stretched between them, filled only by the sound of raindrops hitting the pavement.

The Idea

Ulysses rocked on his heels, then suddenly grinned.

"Wanna run for it?"

She turned to him sharply.

"...What?"
"You know, make a break for it. Sprint home. Bet I’d get there first."

Her expression shifted from annoyed to horrified in an instant.

"No."
"Oh, come on, where’s your sense of adventure?"
"It’s dry and waiting for me at home."
"Booooring," he teased, leaning closer. "C’mon, Leighton, live a little."

She scowled, stepping away.

"I’d rather wait."
"Suit yourself," he said, stretching. "Guess we’re stuck together a little longer. How tragic."

She sighed, muttering something under her breath. He couldn’t hear it, but judging by the look on her face, it wasn’t anything nice.

The Food Stall

The rain showed no signs of stopping, and Ulysses was starting to get restless. He hated sitting still for too long.

He looked around again, then grinned when he spotted a small stall selling street food just a few steps away.

"Hey," he nudged Leighton again. "You hungry?"

She frowned.

"...Why?"
"Because I am. And because that guy over there is selling fish balls. And because you look like you haven’t eaten since last century."

She crossed her arms.

"I’m fine."
"Liar," he said, already stepping away from the awning. "Wait here, I’ll be back."

She didn’t have time to protest before he was already jogging toward the stall, completely ignoring the rain.

Leighton stared after him, her expression caught between disbelief and frustration.

She hated how easily he did things like this—just acting without thinking, not caring about the consequences, not worrying about how other people saw him.

She, on the other hand, overthought everything. It was exhausting.

The Return

By the time Ulysses returned, his hair was dripping, and he was holding up a plastic cup filled with skewers.

"Mission success," he declared proudly, shaking the rain off his sleeves. "Here, take one."

She stared at him, then at the skewers.

"...I didn’t ask for that."
"Yeah, but you’re getting one anyway," he said, holding it out.

She hesitated before sighing and reluctantly taking a stick. The first bite was warm, crispy, and surprisingly comforting.

Ulysses watched her expectantly.

"Well?"

She chewed slowly, then mumbled,

"...It’s okay."

He grinned.

"Knew you’d like it."

She huffed but kept eating.

The rain continued, but somehow, standing there, eating street food under a tiny awning, it didn’t feel so bad.

Ulysses was still annoying, but at least he was bearable.
