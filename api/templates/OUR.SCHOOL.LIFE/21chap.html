Chapter 21: Graduation Day

The graduation day was finally here. The school was buzzing with excitement as students hurried around, making last-minute preparations. The auditorium had been decorated with banners and balloons, and the stage was set for the ceremony. It felt surreal—after all the years of studying, stressing over exams, and dealing with bullies, it was all coming to an end.

Ulysses stood near the entrance, adjusting his graduation cap.

“This thing feels weird,” he muttered, poking at it.

Leonard smirked.

“It’s just a cap. How uncomfortable can it be?”

“You try wearing it,” Ulysses shot back. “It’s like having a square plate glued to your head.”

Leighton walked past them, silently fixing her own cap. She had barely spoken all morning, but that wasn’t surprising. Graduation meant a big crowd, and a big crowd meant more eyes on her. She was already dreading the ceremony.

Christian appeared next, holding a folded piece of paper.

“Did you guys memorize your speeches?”

Fritz groaned.

“Wait, we were supposed to do that?”

“You’re not even one of the speakers,” Daniel pointed out.

“Yeah, but what if I have to give an emergency speech?” Fritz said dramatically.

“Then we’re doomed,” Val muttered, shaking his head.

The teacher called for everyone to line up, signaling that the ceremony was about to begin. Students straightened their uniforms and fixed their caps, trying to look somewhat presentable. Royce and his group stood a little further away, looking as smug as ever. Even on graduation day, they couldn’t help acting superior.

The ceremony started with a few speeches from teachers and the school principal. Most of the students zoned out, clapping when necessary but otherwise waiting for their turn to go on stage.

When it was finally time to receive their diplomas, they lined up by name, one by one stepping up to shake hands with the principal and take their certificates.

Leighton’s turn came, and she hesitated for a split second before walking forward. She could feel hundreds of eyes on her, and her face burned with embarrassment. She quickly took her diploma, bowed slightly, and rushed off the stage as fast as she could.

Ulysses, of course, noticed.

“Wow, I think that’s the fastest I’ve ever seen you move,” he whispered when she returned to her seat.

Leighton shot him a glare but said nothing.

The rest of the ceremony went smoothly, and soon, the students were tossing their caps into the air, cheering. It was finally over.

Planning the Party

After taking a bunch of photos, they regrouped outside to talk about their graduation party.

“We’re holding it at Val’s house, right?” Yeshe asked.

“Yeah, my parents said it’s fine,” Val confirmed. “We just need to buy food and set everything up.”

“Are we inviting everyone?” Daniel looked around.

Ulysses glanced at Royce’s group, who were standing nearby.

“I’d rather not.”

Leonard nodded.

“Agreed.”

They split up into teams—some were in charge of decorations, some were buying food, and others were making sure Val’s house was ready for the party.

Grocery Store Adventures

Leighton was stuck with Ulysses, which was probably the worst possible outcome for her. They were assigned to buy snacks and drinks, which meant she had to follow him around the grocery store while he made ridiculous choices.

“Should we get five packs of chips or ten?” Ulysses asked, holding up two different bags.

“Five,” Leighton said immediately.

Ulysses ignored her and threw ten packs into the cart.

“You’re impossible,” Leighton sighed.

“And you’re predictable,” Ulysses teased. “Come on, it’s a party. We need more snacks.”

They grabbed drinks, candy, and a few other random items before heading to checkout. When they arrived at Val’s house, the others were already there, setting up decorations.

“About time,” Fritz said. “We were starting to think you guys got lost.”

“Leighton wanted to take her time picking out the best snacks,” Ulysses lied.

Leighton’s glare could have burned a hole through him.

The Party Begins

As they set up the food table, Christian and Leonard worked on putting up streamers and balloons. Daniel and Yeshe arranged chairs while Val checked on the music playlist.

By the time everything was ready, the sun was already setting. The party kicked off with loud music, food, and laughter.

Leighton sat in a corner, sipping her drink and watching as the others played games and teased each other. Ulysses eventually walked over, sitting beside her.

“Not bad, right?” he said.

Leighton shrugged.

Ulysses grinned.

“You’re gonna miss us when we’re gone.”

Leighton didn’t respond, but for once, she didn’t throw anything at him.
