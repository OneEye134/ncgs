CHAPTER 13

Disaster in the Making

It all started when the teacher announced that they would be doing a group project, a phrase that instantly sent a wave of groans across the classroom. Everyone knew what group projects really meant—either you got lucky with a team that actually worked, or you ended up with a bunch of slackers who left the workload to one unlucky person.

Unfortunately for Leighton, her luck had never been great when it came to these things.

So when she heard the words, “You’ll be paired with Ulysses,” her entire body stiffened. Her hands gripped the edge of her desk so hard that her knuckles turned white, while across from her, Ulysses only grinned, looking entirely too pleased with himself as he leaned back in his chair and stretched.

“Guess we’re partners, huh?” he said casually, as if he hadn’t been tormenting her with his constant teasing for months now.

Leighton already knew this was going to be a complete nightmare.

“Just don’t mess around,” she muttered, gathering her notes and trying to ignore the way he was watching her like he found her reactions entertaining, which, knowing him, he probably did.

“Mess around? Me?” Ulysses gasped, feigning offense. “Leighton, you wound me. I am a responsible, hardworking student.”

“That’s the biggest lie I’ve ever heard.”

“Ouch.”

She sighed, rubbing her temples, already regretting everything. But before she could say anything else, the teacher continued:

“You’ll be working in groups of four, so I’ll be adding two more students to each pair.”

That caught Leighton’s attention. For a brief moment, she felt hopeful—maybe, just maybe, they’d get paired with someone serious. Someone who would balance out Ulysses' ridiculousness.

But that hope shattered instantly when the teacher called out two more names: and Fritz.

The entire class froze. If Ulysses was a menace, then Daniel and Fritz were absolute disasters. They were the type of students who rarely did homework, never took anything seriously, and spent most of their time cracking jokes in the back of the classroom. Even Ulysses, for all his teasing, was still a decent student when he actually tried, but and Fritz? Lost causes.

Leighton felt actual despair settle in her chest as the two boys sauntered over, grinning like they’d just won the lottery.

“Yo, we’re in the same group?” Daniel laughed, throwing an arm around Ulysses’ shoulder. “This is gonna be fun.”

“Or a disaster,” Leighton muttered under her breath.

Fritz, who had flopped down into a chair with zero care for posture, smirked.

“C’mon, Leighton, have some faith. We’re totally capable of doing a project.”

“Since when?”

“Since now,” Fritz said shamelessly, grinning.

Ulysses, meanwhile, was just enjoying the chaos, clearly finding her frustration hilarious.

“So, when are we meeting up to work on this thing?” he asked.

Before Leighton could open her mouth, Daniel spoke first.

“Let’s do it at Leighton’s house.”

She choked.

“What—no!”

“Why not?” Fritz smirked. “You got something to hide?”

“I just don’t want a bunch of idiots wrecking my house,” she snapped, though her ears were turning red, and Ulysses, of course, noticed.

“Aww, Leighton, are you embarrassed to have us over?”

“Shut up.”

“That’s a yes.”

“I hate all of you.”

Unfortunately, Leighton had no choice in the matter. The majority had already decided.

By the time the school day ended, she found herself standing in her living room, staring in absolute horror as three boys made themselves at home.

Fritz had already raided the fridge, Daniel was playing with random objects like a child, and Ulysses? He was lounging on her couch like he owned the place, arms behind his head, smirking at her growing frustration.

“Alright, let’s get started,” he said, not even trying to hide his amusement. “Where’s the food?”

Leighton exhaled slowly, clutching the project guidelines in her trembling hands. This was going to be the longest study session of her life.
