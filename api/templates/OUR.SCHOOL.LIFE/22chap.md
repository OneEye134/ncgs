[chapter-22-final-meeting]# Chapter 22: Final Meeting

The day after graduation, Ulysses and his friends decided to spend the whole day hanging out. They had planned everything—video games, carnival rides, and street food. It was supposed to be the perfect way to celebrate, but as expected, Leighton wasn’t thrilled about it.

---

[video-game-chaos]## Video Game Chaos

At Christian’s house, the group crowded around the TV, controllers in hand, as they played a chaotic multiplayer game. The room was filled with shouting and laughter as they battled each other, trying to win.

> "Stop spamming the same move!" Fritz yelled at Daniel, who was mashing buttons with a smug grin.
> "It’s not my fault you keep falling for it," Daniel shot back.

Yeshe groaned as his character got knocked out.

> "I swear, this game is rigged."

Meanwhile, Leighton sat at the edge of the couch, controller in her hands but barely doing anything. She wasn’t bad at video games, but she also didn’t care enough to try. She mostly just watched as Ulysses and the others got competitive.

Ulysses nudged her.

> "You’re just standing there. Do something."

Leighton sighed and pressed a random button. Her character immediately got knocked off the stage.

> "Wow, great strategy," Ulysses teased.

She handed him the controller.

> "You play for me."

Ulysses smirked but didn’t argue. He took over, getting way too into the game, while Leighton leaned back and stared at the ceiling, already waiting for the day to be over.

---

[carnival-chaos]## Carnival Chaos

After a few more matches, they left the house and headed to the carnival.

The place was packed with people, bright lights flashing from different booths and rides. The smell of popcorn and fried food filled the air, and excited screams echoed from the roller coaster.

> "This is going to be fun!" Fritz said excitedly.

Leighton wasn’t convinced.

They went on a bunch of rides—bumper cars, the Ferris wheel, even the spinning teacups just for the chaos of it. Ulysses and the others had the time of their lives, but Leighton looked miserable the entire time.

> "You’re not scared, are you?" Ulysses asked after she hesitated to get on another ride.

Leighton shot him a glare.

> "I just don’t like this."
> "You don’t like anything," Ulysses muttered, but he let it go.

Instead, they played carnival games. Leonard somehow managed to win a giant stuffed bear, which he proudly carried around. Christian and Val failed horribly at the ring toss, while Daniel and Yeshe spent way too much money trying to win prizes.

---

[trouble-in-the-alleyway]## Trouble in the Alleyway

As the night went on, they finally decided to head home.

They walked down a quiet street, laughing about the events of the day, when suddenly, a group of figures stepped out from the shadows of an alleyway.

Royce and his gang.

> "Leaving so soon?" Royce said with a smirk, blocking their path.

The group immediately tensed. They had just graduated—why was this still happening?

Ulysses stepped forward.

> "Move."

Royce chuckled.

> "Nah. Not yet."

Before anyone could react, one of the bullies grabbed Christian by the collar and shoved him against the wall. Another one pushed Fritz down.

Chaos erupted. Royce’s group started swinging punches, shoving and kicking. Ulysses fought back, but he was outnumbered.

Leighton, who had been standing frozen in shock, barely had time to react before someone grabbed her wrist and yanked her forward.

> "Let go—" she barely managed to say before a fist hit her stomach, knocking the air out of her.

She stumbled back, gasping, pain shooting through her body. She wanted to run, but there was nowhere to go.

Ulysses, seeing this, tried to fight his way toward her, but Royce intercepted him, shoving him hard against the alley wall.

> "This is our last chance to teach you a lesson," Royce sneered.

The beating continued for what felt like forever. Every time one of them tried to get up, they were knocked back down. Leighton curled up on the ground, arms shielding herself as best as she could.

---

[interrupted]## Interrupted

Then, suddenly—

> "Hey! What’s going on here?!"

A voice echoed through the alleyway.

A teacher.

Royce and his group immediately stopped. The teacher, looking furious, marched toward them, and for the first time, Royce actually looked nervous.

Royce clicked his tongue, glaring down at Ulysses.

> "You got lucky," he muttered. Then he leaned in, voice low. "We’ll meet again next year."

With that, Royce and his gang disappeared into the darkness.

Ulysses groaned, sitting up, wiping blood from his lip. The others slowly got to their feet, bruised and exhausted.

The teacher demanded answers, but no one said anything. They just wanted to go home.

---

[the-walk-home]## The Walk Home

After making sure everyone was okay, they walked together in silence.

Eventually, Fritz sighed.

> "Well. That sucked."

Christian winced as he touched a bruise on his face.

> "At least it’s over."

Leighton didn’t say anything. She was still shaken, staring down at her scraped hands.

Ulysses glanced at her.

> "You good?"

She hesitated before nodding.

They walked for a while, the night air cooling their aching bodies.

> "Graduation, a party, and a beatdown all in one weekend," Yeshe muttered. "What a way to end the year."

They all laughed weakly.

---

[the-final-goodbye]## The Final Goodbye

When they finally reached their homes, they stopped outside, standing there for a long moment.

No one wanted to be the first to leave.

> "So, this is it, huh?" Val said quietly.
> "Guess so," Leonard replied.

One by one, they said their goodbyes. A few handshakes, a few pats on the back.

Ulysses turned to Leighton.

She looked at him for a moment before mumbling,

> "See you."

Ulysses smirked.

> "See you."

And with that, they all went their separate ways.
