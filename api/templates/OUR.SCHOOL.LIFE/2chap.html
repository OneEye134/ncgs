CHAPTER 2

Leighton liked quiet places. She liked the stillness of her room, the faint hum of the electric fan, the feeling of being alone with her thoughts. But life had a way of forcing her into loud, chaotic situations, especially when Ulysses was involved.

Today was one of those days. She had been hoping for a peaceful afternoon, maybe some reading or sketching, but instead, she found herself standing in the middle of a crowded neighborhood market, feeling utterly overwhelmed. The place was packed, filled with the chatter of vendors, the clatter of carts, and the strong aroma of spices, grilled meat, and fresh produce. It was the kind of place where everyone knew each other, where bargaining was a sport, and where privacy was nonexistent. And, unfortunately for Leighton, Ulysses had dragged her here under the pretense of "helping him buy groceries."

She didn’t believe for a second that he actually needed her help.

“C’mon, Leighton, don’t look so miserable,” Ulysses said, grinning as he led the way through the narrow aisles. “It’s just shopping.”

Leighton shot him a look that very clearly said, "I hate you."

He laughed.

“You look like a lost kitten.”

She rolled her eyes and adjusted the strap of her bag, trying to focus on not bumping into people. Ulysses, on the other hand, seemed perfectly at ease, weaving through the crowd effortlessly. He stopped at a vegetable stall, casually picking up a tomato and examining it like he actually knew what he was doing.

“How do you even tell if this is good?” he muttered.

Leighton sighed, reaching over to press her fingers against the tomato’s skin. Slightly firm, no blemishes. She gave him a pointed look, as if to say, "Like this."

“Ohhh,” he said, nodding in mock understanding. “So, you’re a vegetable expert now?”

She huffed and turned away. Just as she did, a familiar voice rang through the air.

“Well, well, look who it is.”

Leighton froze. Nathan, Greg, and Oliver. Again. Because, of course, her luck was terrible. They were standing near a fruit stall, smirking like they owned the place. Leighton immediately tensed, gripping her bag strap tightly. She really wasn’t in the mood for this.

“Didn’t expect to see you two here,” Nathan said, crossing his arms. “Wait, don’t tell me—you guys are on a little date?”

Greg whistled.

“Wow, Leighton, I didn’t know you had it in you.”

Ulysses turned around slowly, his expression unreadable.

“Oh, great,” he said flatly. “The three stooges.”

Oliver scoffed.

“Relax, man. We were just saying hi.”

“Yeah,” Greg added. “No need to get all defensive.”

Ulysses raised an eyebrow.

“Oh, so you just naturally sound like jerks? My bad, I thought you were doing it on purpose.”

Leighton almost choked on air. That was brutal. Nathan’s smirk twitched, clearly annoyed.

“You always got something to say, don’t you?”

“Only when I see something stupid,” Ulysses replied smoothly. “Which, in your case, is all the time.”

Greg and Oliver snickered.
At their own friend.

Nathan, looking irritated, clicked his tongue.

“Whatever. Let’s go.”

He shot Leighton a look before turning away.

“See you around, mouse.”

Leighton clenched her fists, but she didn’t say anything. As soon as they were gone, Ulysses sighed dramatically.

“Man, those guys are persistent. I swear, they must have nothing better to do.”

Leighton still felt a little tense, but she exhaled slowly, trying to shake it off. Ulysses glanced at her.

“You okay?”

She hesitated, then gave a small nod.

“Good,” he said, before smirking. “Because we still have a lot of shopping left to do.”

Leighton groaned.

An hour later, they were still at the market. Ulysses had bought way too many unnecessary things, including snacks he definitely didn’t need, a wooden spoon for no reason, and a tiny potted plant that he claimed “looked lonely.” Leighton had long since given up trying to question his decisions.

They stopped at a small street food stall near the exit, where an old man was grilling skewers of meat. The smell was incredible.

“Want some?” Ulysses asked, already pulling out his wallet.

Leighton hesitated. She did want some, but she didn’t want to owe him anything. He seemed to read her thoughts.

“Relax, it’s my treat,” he said, grinning. “Consider it payment for being my personal vegetable inspector.”

She narrowed her eyes, but after a moment, she gave a small nod. He handed her a skewer, and they stood there eating in comfortable silence. The sun was starting to set, casting everything in a warm, golden glow. It was one of those rare moments where Ulysses wasn’t teasing her, where he wasn’t being obnoxious.

Just two people, quietly eating food together. It was… nice.

Leighton glanced at him from the corner of her eye.
Maybe, just maybe, he wasn’t so bad.
