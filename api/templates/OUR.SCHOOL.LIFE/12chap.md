[chapter-12]# CHAPTER 12

[chapter-12-market-chaos]## Aisles of Chaos

It started as a simple errand—Leighton needed to buy a few things from the local market, and somehow, Ulysses invited himself along, which, in hindsight, was probably her first mistake because shopping with Ulysses was never going to be a peaceful experience, and she really should have known better by now, but before she could protest, he was already walking beside her, hands in his pockets, grinning like he had nothing better to do.

> "What do you even need?" he asked as they entered the crowded market, the scent of freshly baked bread and grilled street food filling the air, mingling with the chatter of vendors calling out their prices and customers bargaining loudly.

Leighton pulled out her neatly folded shopping list and mumbled,

> "Vegetables… rice… eggs…"

Before she could tuck it away, Ulysses's fingers twitched, clearly tempted to snatch it from her hands.

> "Boring," Ulysses said, stretching. "Where’s the fun stuff? No snacks? No candy?"
> <br/>
> "I’m here to buy groceries, not junk food," she replied, already heading toward the vegetable stalls, hoping he would lose interest and wander off somewhere else.

Of course, he followed her like a persistent shadow.

> "Lame," he sighed dramatically, before suddenly grabbing a random vegetable and holding it up. "What is this?"
> <br/>
> "... A cabbage," Leighton said, staring.
> <br/>
> "Are you sure? It looks weird."
> <br/>
> "That’s because you’re holding it upside down."

He blinked, slowly flipping it.

> "... Oh."

She sighed, rubbing her temples. This was going to be a long day.

[chaos-at-the-market]### Chaos at the Market

As Leighton inspected the tomatoes, pressing each one carefully, Ulysses picked one up and cleared his throat dramatically.

> "Fresh, juicy tomatoes! Guaranteed to make you cry tears of joy or your money back!"

She gave him a flat look.

> "Stop that."
> <br/>
> "Oh, come on, Leighton, where’s your sense of fun? You’re acting like a stressed-out mom shopping for a family of five."
> <br/>
> "I’d be less stressed if you weren’t here."
> <br/>
> "Ouch. That hurts, you know."
> <br/>
> "Good."

Ulysses laughed, but before he could tease her more, a commotion broke out near the seafood section—a loud shout, followed by the unmistakable splash of water.

Curious, Ulysses turned toward the noise just in time to see a young boy running past, a large fish clutched in his tiny hands, while an angry vendor chased after him, waving a ladle.

> "Hey! Come back here, you little thief!"

Ulysses blinked.

> "Huh. Didn’t think I’d see a heist today."

Leighton, however, looked worried.

> "Should we… stop him?"
> <br/>
> "Nah, I wanna see how this plays out."
> <br/>
> "Ulysses."

He sighed dramatically before stepping forward just as the boy darted past again. With quick reflexes, he grabbed the kid by the collar, lifting him off the ground.

> "Alright, buddy, time to return the stolen goods."

The boy struggled fiercely, kicking his legs.

> "Let me go! I need this fish!"

The vendor caught up, panting.

> "You little brat! Always stealing from me! I should—"
> "Wait," Leighton cut in, her voice unusually firm. She looked between the boy and the vendor. "Why did you take it?"

The boy hesitated before muttering,

> "For my little sister. She’s sick. I wanted to make her soup."

Leighton’s expression softened, while Ulysses sighed and set the kid down.

> "Look, stealing isn’t the way to go," Ulysses said, ruffling the boy’s messy hair. "But I get it. How about this—we pay for the fish, and you promise not to do it again?"

The vendor crossed his arms, grumbling.

> "As long as I get my money, I don’t care who pays."

Leighton quickly pulled out some money, handing it over. The boy stared up at them in shock.

> "... You’re really paying for me?"
> <br/>
> "Just don’t do it again," Leighton mumbled, looking away, her ears turning pink.

The boy nodded quickly, clutching the fish to his chest before running off. As the vendor walked away, Ulysses smirked.

> "Look at you, Leighton. Secretly a hero, huh?"

She shoved a bag of onions at him.

> "Carry this and shut up."

He laughed, taking the bag as they continued shopping. But as much as Leighton tried to focus on the task at hand, she couldn’t ignore the small, warm feeling that lingered in her chest.

Maybe shopping with Ulysses wasn’t so bad after all.
